# Becoming My Own Home - Chapter 1

This is the first chapter of my journey in becoming my own home.

![Chapter 1 Image](/assets/images/becoming-my-own-home/chapter1.jpeg)

## Chapter 1: When It All Started

It was January last year when my roommate flew back to her home country for a 45-day vacation, leaving the apartment unusually quiet under the softest winter sun along with a bunch of plants for me to take care of. For the first time in a while, I had the space entirely to myself — no late-night conversations, no background music from the next room, just silence. And that silence was pretty awkward, as it was the first time I was living alone in a huge apartment. But as the days went by, I started to settle in, learning to enjoy my own company, noticing every single thing around me that I would have never noticed if I was not alone. It was altogether like cooking for one and finding comfort in the quiet.

My apartment was facing the lake, so I used to get this beautiful sunrise by the lake view. One fine wintery day under the bright sun, I was sitting in my living room and sipping a warm cup of coffee. I was wondering how important it is to enjoy your own company and what it means to your future self. Then I slowly started my journaling — mind you, I used to journal when I was 8. It always brings back my memories of a little kid sitting in the corner of the balcony, getting all creative and jotting those thoughts down in the heavy diary. Haha! Fun days. I was always the kind of girl who loves fitness, hanging out with friends, meeting new people and talking about their life experiences, always curious to learn new things. Well, never have I realized until that day how grateful I am to sit in my own apartment in one of the most gorgeous cities in the world and sip my coffee. There is beauty in taking a minute from your busy day to stop, look around, and be grateful for things we have. In the generation of hustle, always being busy with random things, we forget to appreciate the things which we have achieved — these are the things which we once prayed for. Yes, we are the creatures who always think about the future, what's next to achieve, but we tend to forget to feel the joy of "being in the present" and also how important it is to know yourself, love yourself, and spend time with yourself. 'Cause how else would you know and understand another being when you can't even tell "who you are?"

A little bit about me — I am a girlie who does a vision board every year; it's like a New Year's ritual. That particular year, I decided to include one thing every year which makes you better — not in ambition, not in health, but in becoming a better person. This year it was "do one selfless thing whenever you can which brings a smile to a person's face; it could be a stranger too." I started to invest my time more in reading and watching the self love journals, you know when you are at home when you feel welcomed, cozy and safe so why not be that for yourself and trust me it is so easy to say and relatively difficult to do. As I step into this journey there were few things which needed attention so here's my list which I made to get myself started in becoming my own home:

- Cleanse your energy
- Gratitude for the life I have and positive thinking even when the times are tough
- Eat clean and exercise regularly remember body is your temple worship it
- Resume writing and reading
- Consume productive content
- Don't forget to celebrate small wins
- And this one is my favorite "Pamper yourself"

The moment I started to focus on my list I have noticed a major shift in my energy, well when I say "Energy" I meant the way you see and perceive things and situations, the type of people you attract, it did not happen overnight it was gradual. The way you look at things changes drastically and you stop looking at stuff with your judgy little eyes and start seeing the bigger picture and trust me not every time you have to react I'd say respond rather reacting.

A few weeks later, I found myself sitting in a cozy café, my hands wrapped around a warm cup of latte. As I watched people hurry past outside, I glanced down at the list I'd written that quiet morning in my apartment and started wondering "this literally seems so simple and impossible at the same time". These few practices were supposed to change everything. Duh? Honestly, I had no idea how profoundly those simple practices would reshape my life. What started as intentions scribbled during a quiet winter morning was becoming the foundation of a future version of me.

Over the months that followed each item on that list revealed a newer and a better version of myself and undoubtedly there were lessons learnt few of them were gentle and others were uncomfortable but all were necessary because this wasn't about just self improvement journey or checking boxes but it was all about unlearning old patterns, building a relationship with myself and discovering what it truly means to feels to be your own home in your own skin. This is not just an article but my journey in Becoming My Own Home!
